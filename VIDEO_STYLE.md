# Dein-Dein Video Style

This document is the canonical motion and visual system for Dein-Dein
feature-promotion videos. It applies the rules in
[MARKETING_BRAND_GUIDE.md](MARKETING_BRAND_GUIDE.md) specifically to video.

## Default Creative Direction

Create premium, product-led promotional reels that show how Dein-Dein helps
people discover Indian movies, cultural events, and community experiences in
Germany.

The style is modern and energetic, but clarity is more important than spectacle.
Use real product recordings as proof, and use motion to focus attention on the
feature being explained.

## Default Format

| Property | Default |
| --- | --- |
| Canvas | `1080x1920`, vertical `9:16` |
| Duration | Approximately `20 seconds` |
| Frame rate | `30 fps` |
| Subject | One feature and one audience benefit |
| Source | Real screen recording |
| Audio | Rights-cleared music; restrained interaction accents |
| Accessibility | Burned-in captions; understandable while muted |
| Ending | Stable brand frame held for at least `1.5 seconds` |

## Story Rhythm

Use this default structure unless the approved storyboard requires a different
timing:

| Time | Purpose | Typical Treatment |
| --- | --- | --- |
| `0:00-0:02` | Hook | Short question, need, or benefit; immediately readable |
| `0:02-0:06` | Context | Establish the relevant product screen and user intent |
| `0:06-0:14` | Feature proof | Show the real interaction and focus on the useful result |
| `0:14-0:18` | Benefit | Resolve the hook with concise product value |
| `0:18-0:20` | CTA | Official logo, one approved CTA, and `dein-dein.com` |

The timing is a guide, not permission to rush copy. Every title and caption must
remain readable on a phone.

## Visual System

### Palette

- Use Deep Navy `#1A1432` and Dein Navy `#241C46` as the dominant fields.
- Use Dein Gold `#F0C75E` for focused emphasis, progress, markers, and CTAs.
- Use Dein Plum `#8A6DD1` only as restrained atmosphere or secondary emphasis.
- Use Soft Ivory `#F6F1E8` for primary text on dark fields.
- Use Warm Canvas `#F7F2EA` or Warm Ivory surfaces for calm light cards.

Do not replace the palette with a generic neon-tech treatment. Subtle glows are
acceptable when they use approved gold or plum and improve focus.

### Typography

- Use FK Grotesk Neue when available, with Inter or a clean modern grotesk as
  fallback.
- Keep captions concise, high-contrast, and within vertical safe areas.
- Use no more than three type sizes and two font weights in one reel.
- Use kinetic typography only for short hooks or transitions; words must remain
  readable and stable.
- Highlight a maximum of one key phrase at a time.

### Product Footage

- Preserve the real UI and its truthful sequence.
- Present recordings inside a clean crop, rounded viewport, or restrained
  device frame.
- Use navy overlays or surrounding fields to improve focus and legibility.
- Magnify the relevant interaction rather than showing an unreadably small full
  screen.
- Keep pointer movement purposeful and avoid emphasizing accidental cursor
  motion.

Do not fabricate product states, replace labels, alter facts, or generate a fake
interface to make the feature appear more capable.

## Motion Language

### Preferred Motion

- Smooth crop and pan toward the active UI region
- Short, purposeful zooms that settle before the next action
- Gentle scale from `96%` to `100%`
- Clean fades and upward reveals
- Masked screen-recording reveals
- Subtle parallax between product footage and supporting labels
- Soft gold or plum focus light
- Cuts and transitions aligned to musical phrasing

### Motion Restraint

- Use one dominant transition idea per reel.
- Let important screens settle long enough to understand.
- Reserve the fastest movement for scene changes, not factual details.
- Keep the closing frame still after its reveal.

Avoid rapid flashing, constant zooming, excessive glitch effects, spinning
type, bouncing logo parts, and motion that makes text or UI difficult to read.

## Captions and On-Screen Copy

- The full story must remain understandable with audio muted.
- Prefer one short sentence or phrase per scene.
- Write direct, useful copy focused on the audience benefit.
- Keep factual UI labels and names exactly as shown or supplied.
- Do not place text over important UI controls, faces, or dense imagery.
- Use a dark readable panel, gradient, or clear negative space behind captions.

Use an approved primary CTA from the marketing brand guide. `Book Now` is not a
default CTA and requires explicit approval under the rules in [AGENTS.md](AGENTS.md).

## Audio Direction

- Use only rights-cleared music, voice, and sound effects.
- Choose music with a modern, warm, forward-moving pulse rather than aggressive
  club energy.
- Keep sound effects subtle and tied to meaningful interactions or transitions.
- Mix audio so captions are never optional for comprehension.
- Do not use trending audio without confirmed commercial usage rights.

## Logo and Closing Frame

- Use the official logo file intact.
- Reveal it with a fade, short upward movement, or gentle `96%` to `100%` scale.
- Keep the logo still and readable after the reveal.
- Pair it with one approved CTA and `dein-dein.com`.
- Hold the closing frame for at least `1.5 seconds`.

Do not glow, recolor, crop, distort, regenerate, or animate the internal logo
geometry.

## Format Adaptation

Vertical reels are the production default. Adapt to landscape or square only
when required by an approved campaign brief. Recompose the product footage and
captions for the target canvas; do not stretch or mechanically crop the final
vertical export.

## Creative Test

Before approving a style choice, ask:

1. Does it make the feature easier to understand?
2. Does it look and sound like Dein-Dein?
3. Is the product behavior truthful?
4. Is it readable on a phone and understandable while muted?
5. Does it respect privacy, rights, and the approved CTA?

If the answer to any question is no, revise the choice.
