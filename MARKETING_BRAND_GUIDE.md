# Dein-Dein Marketing Brand Guide

This guide is the source of truth for people and AI tools creating Dein-Dein
promotional posters, videos, reels, social posts, thumbnails, and campaign
assets.

The website supports configurable themes. Marketing assets should use the
stable brand identity defined here unless a campaign brief explicitly approves
another treatment.

## Brand Foundation

### What Dein-Dein Is

Dein-Dein is an independent discovery platform for Indian movie showtimes,
cultural events, and community gatherings in Germany.

It helps people answer practical questions:

- What Indian movies and events are happening?
- When and where are they happening?
- Which city, cinema, language, or community are they relevant to?
- Where can people confirm details or book through the official provider?

Dein-Dein supports discovery. It is not the cinema, event organizer, or
ticketing provider unless a specific campaign brief says otherwise.

### Canonical Name

| Context | Use | Example |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Visible headlines, prose, captions, and spoken references | **Dein-Dein** | `Discover it on Dein-Dein` |
| URLs, social handles, technical identifiers, and legal contexts that already use lowercase | **dein-dein** | `dein-dein.com` |
| New marketing assets | Never use **Event Dein** | `Event Dein` is a legacy name |

Do not write `Dein Dein`, `DeinDein`, or `DEIN-DEIN` as the primary visible
brand name. The all-caps form may appear only in small typographic treatments,
not as a replacement for the canonical name.

### Official Destinations

Use only these repository-confirmed destinations unless a campaign brief
provides another approved link:

- Website: [dein-dein.com](https://dein-dein.com)
- Instagram: [@dein_dein_official](https://www.instagram.com/dein_dein_official/)
- Facebook: [facebook.com/deindeincom](https://www.facebook.com/deindeincom/)
- X: [@deindein13](https://x.com/deindein13)

Do not invent TikTok, YouTube, campaign, or regional handles.

### Motto

> **You. Your Movies. Your Events.**

Use the motto when the asset is about Dein-Dein itself, broad discovery, or
community belonging. Do not force it into event-specific assets when event
details and a clear call to action need the space.

### Primary Audience

- People in Germany looking for Indian-language movie showtimes
- People looking for Indian cultural events and community gatherings
- Organizers who want their event to reach a relevant audience
- Communities across languages, cities, ages, and cultural backgrounds

Marketing should feel locally useful and culturally aware without reducing the
audience to stereotypes.

### Brand Personality

Dein-Dein is:

- **Useful:** concrete details and clear next steps come first.
- **Welcoming:** community-oriented, inclusive, and easy to understand.
- **Energetic:** alive with cinema, culture, and shared experiences.
- **Trustworthy:** accurate, transparent, and careful about uncertain details.
- **Premium but approachable:** polished without feeling exclusive or distant.
- **Independent:** helpful to audiences without pretending to be the organizer
  or ticket seller.

Avoid generic corporate language, exaggerated hype, clickbait, and claims that
cannot be verified.

## Messaging System

### Messaging Pillars

1. **Discovery made practical:** find relevant movies and events by city, date,
   and language.
2. **Culture and community:** bring people closer to shared experiences.
3. **One useful destination:** reduce the work needed to find what is happening.
4. **Confidence before action:** encourage people to confirm final details with
   the official cinema, organizer, or ticketing provider.

### Voice and Tone

Use short, direct, warm language. Lead with the audience benefit or the event.
Prefer active verbs and specific details.

| Prefer | Avoid |
| --- | --- |
| `Find Tamil movie showtimes in Berlin.` | `Unlock an unparalleled cinematic journey.` |
| `See what is happening this weekend.` | `The ultimate event ecosystem is here.` |
| `Confirm times and tickets with the official provider.` | `Guaranteed tickets available now.` |
| `Discover Indian movies and events across Germany.` | `Germany's number one platform.` |

Use title case sparingly. Headlines may use sentence case or concise title
case. Do not use long passages in all caps.

### Approved Call-to-Action Patterns

Use one primary call to action per asset.

- `Discover on Dein-Dein`
- `Find showtimes`
- `Explore events`
- `See what is happening`
- `View event details`
- `Check dates and cities`
- `Find events near you`
- `Confirm and book with the official provider`
- `Visit dein-dein.com`

Do not use `Book now`, `Buy tickets`, `Sold out soon`, or similar urgency unless
the destination, availability, and wording are explicitly confirmed in the
campaign brief.

## Logo

### Approved Assets

Primary logo:

![Primary Dein-Dein logo](dein-new-logo.png)

- Source: `dein-new-logo.png`
- Use: all new marketing and promotional assets
- Format: square transparent PNG, 1254 x 1254 pixels, with the original navy
  and gold mark plus a subtle outer shadow
- Archived opaque source: `assets/archive/dein-new-logo-opaque-original.png`;
  provenance only, not approved for new work

Legacy logo:

- Source: not stored in this repository
- Do not use in new marketing assets.

The primary mark combines a deep navy rounded shape with two gold geometric
forms. Treat the complete supplied image as the logo. Do not redraw or
reinterpret its internal geometry.

### Logo Usage Rules

- Preserve the original aspect ratio.
- Keep clear space around the logo equal to at least one quarter of the visible
  logo mark's width.
- Use at least 48 x 48 pixels in digital assets and 15 mm wide in print.
- Place it in a calm corner, header, or closing frame where it remains legible.
- On busy imagery, place the complete logo image on a simple light card or
  increase the surrounding clear space.
- The transparent background may blend into approved light or dark brand
  backdrops; preserve the complete canvas and clear space.
- For small placements, pair it with the text `Dein-Dein` when brand recognition
  may be weak.

Never:

- Stretch, squash, crop, rotate, skew, or mirror the logo.
- Remove, recolor, regenerate, or animate individual internal shapes.
- Add an outline, glow, texture, filter, or color overlay to the logo.
- Replace the approved transparent background with a baked-in color.
- Place copy, stickers, or partner marks inside its clear space.
- Use an AI-generated approximation of the logo.

### Video Logo Behavior

- Prefer a clean fade, gentle scale from 96% to 100%, or short upward reveal.
- Keep a closing logo frame visible for at least 1.5 seconds.
- Keep the complete logo still and readable after the reveal.
- Do not make the internal gold shapes bounce, spin, separate, or morph.

## Color System

### Stable Marketing Palette

These colors are derived from the shared theme contract and are the stable
palette for marketing work.

| Role | Name | Hex | Use |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Primary | Dein Navy | `#241C46` | Main dark field, headings, frames, logo-aligned identity |
| Primary dark | Deep Navy | `#1A1432` | Rich backgrounds, gradients, high-contrast video frames |
| Accent | Dein Gold | `#F0C75E` | Calls to action, key words, dates, lines, and highlights |
| Accent dark | Deep Gold | `#C79934` | Gold depth, subtle borders, secondary accents |
| Secondary accent | Dein Plum | `#8A6DD1` | Restrained cultural energy, ambient light, secondary emphasis |
| Light background | Warm Canvas | `#F7F2EA` | Light layouts and calm negative space |
| Light surface | Warm Ivory | `rgba(255, 249, 241, 0.90)` | Cards and readable light panels |
| Dark text | Ink | `#1E1737` | Text on light backgrounds |
| Light text | Soft Ivory | `#F6F1E8` | Text on dark backgrounds |
| Muted light text | Soft Ivory 72% | `rgba(246, 241, 232, 0.72)` | Supporting text on dark backgrounds |

The application dark theme currently uses sapphire blue in some interactive
contexts. Do not replace the stable marketing plum with sapphire unless a
campaign brief explicitly calls for a digital or technology-led treatment.

### Recommended Color Proportions

For a typical Dein-Dein-owned asset:

- 60-75% navy, deep navy, cinematic imagery, or warm canvas
- 15-25% readable neutral surface or negative space
- 5-10% gold emphasis
- 0-8% plum atmosphere or secondary emphasis

Gold is an accent, not a full-page default. Plum should support the composition,
not compete with gold.

### Approved Gradient Directions

Use gradients as atmospheric support, not as the subject.

```text
Primary dark:
linear-gradient(135deg, #1A1432 0%, #241C46 55%, #3B2464 100%)

Gold focus:
radial-gradient(circle, rgba(240,199,94,0.38) 0%, rgba(240,199,94,0) 70%)

Plum atmosphere:
radial-gradient(circle, rgba(138,109,209,0.32) 0%, rgba(138,109,209,0) 72%)
```

Avoid rainbow gradients, neon multicolor palettes, equal-weight gold and plum
fields, or low-contrast gold body text on light backgrounds.

## Typography

### Primary Typeface

Use **FK Grotesk Neue** for Dein-Dein-owned typography when an approved font
file is available. The primary web application uses FK Grotesk Neue at weights
400-700, but the font file is not currently stored in this repository.

Practical fallback stack:

```css
"FK Grotesk Neue", "Inter", system-ui, -apple-system, BlinkMacSystemFont,
"Segoe UI", sans-serif
```

When FK Grotesk Neue cannot be licensed, embedded, or accessed by a production
tool, use Inter or a clean modern grotesk. Do not imitate FK Grotesk Neue by
distorting another font.

### Hierarchy

- **Hero headline:** bold, compact, high contrast, usually one to three lines
- **Kicker or category:** small uppercase text with generous letter spacing
- **Details:** medium-weight, highly readable date, city, venue, and language
- **Supporting copy:** regular weight with short line lengths
- **Call to action:** semibold, concise, and visually separated

Use no more than three type sizes and two font weights in a small social asset.
Keep essential copy inside platform-safe areas and readable on a phone.

## Visual Language

### Core Direction

Dein-Dein marketing should combine:

- Cinematic movie or event imagery
- Warm human and community energy
- Premium dark navy surfaces
- Focused gold highlights
- Restrained plum ambient accents
- Readable overlays with strong contrast
- Rounded geometry inspired by the logo
- Subtle depth through soft shadows, light bloom, and layered surfaces

The result should feel like a useful cultural discovery guide, not a generic
ticket marketplace, corporate conference brand, or nightclub flyer.

### Imagery

- Prefer authentic event, cinema, venue, city, or community photography when
  rights are confirmed.
- When generating people, represent the relevant community naturally and avoid
  tokenism, caricatures, or stereotypical clothing unless the event context
  specifically requires it.
- Keep skin tones natural and faces anatomically credible.
- Use cinematic crops with a clear focal subject and enough negative space for
  copy.
- Apply navy overlays or gradients behind text rather than placing text
  directly on visually noisy areas.
- Do not generate or imply celebrity likenesses, movie characters, studio
  artwork, or copyrighted posters without explicit rights.

### Graphic Motifs

- Rounded rectangles and asymmetric rounded panels inspired by the logo
- Fine gold dividers, small gold markers, or restrained pill-shaped labels
- Soft plum or gold radial light
- Subtle grid, scanline, or terminal-inspired detail for discovery-led assets
- Layered cards for dates, cities, languages, or showtimes

Do not overload a composition with every motif. Choose one dominant visual idea
and one supporting motif.

## Composition by Format

### Event Poster

- Recommended ratios: `4:5` for feeds and `A-series` for print.
- Lead with event name or primary audience benefit.
- Show date, city, venue, and primary call to action as a clear second layer.
- Keep the Dein-Dein logo subordinate to the promoted event unless Dein-Dein is
  the subject.
- Reserve a clean lower or upper area for confirmed partner and organizer marks.

### Story or Reel

- Ratio: `9:16`.
- Keep critical text away from the top and bottom interface zones.
- Communicate one idea per scene.
- Show the hook in the first two seconds and the call to action in the final
  scene.
- Use captions for all meaningful spoken content.

### Landscape Video

- Ratio: `16:9`.
- Keep text and logos within the central title-safe area.
- Use wider environmental or event imagery and avoid stretching vertical assets.
- End with the logo, `Dein-Dein`, the primary call to action, and
  `dein-dein.com`.

### Thumbnail

- Design for recognition at small size.
- Use one focal image, one short headline, and one brand cue.
- Avoid schedules, paragraphs, multiple logos, or more than five to seven words
  of headline copy.

### Social Card

- Default ratio: `1:1` or `4:5`.
- Use a strong image, short headline, one useful detail cluster, and one call to
  action.
- Use navy as the frame or overlay, gold for the key detail, and plum only as
  atmosphere.

## Motion and Video

- Keep motion purposeful, smooth, and restrained.
- Prefer fades, short slides, gentle scale, masked image reveals, and subtle
  parallax.
- Use cuts or transitions that support the music and information hierarchy.
- Keep title cards visible long enough to read twice at a comfortable pace.
- Reveal date, city, venue, and call to action in a stable information block.
- Use burned-in captions with strong contrast and safe margins.
- Respect reduced-motion needs in interactive exports.
- Use only music, voice, footage, and sound effects with confirmed usage rights.

Avoid fast flashing, constant zooming, excessive glitch effects, spinning type,
kinetic text that cannot be read, and motion that changes factual details.

## Accuracy, Rights, and Safety

Every marketing asset must:

- Use only event names, dates, times, cities, venues, prices, languages,
  availability, URLs, and calls to action supplied by a trusted source.
- Mark unknown details as pending or omit them. Never invent missing facts.
- Avoid claims of guaranteed availability, sales, popularity, or outcomes.
- Direct audiences to confirm final details with the official cinema, organizer,
  or ticketing provider when appropriate.
- Use third-party logos, artwork, movie stills, music, footage, and photographs
  only when permission or an applicable license is confirmed.
- Avoid misleading edits, discriminatory imagery, unsafe behavior, sexualized
  depictions of minors, graphic violence, and culturally insensitive imagery.
- Preserve readable contrast, captions, and non-color cues for essential
  information.

## Final Consistency Checklist

Before approving an asset, confirm:

- [ ] The visible brand name is `Dein-Dein`; `Event Dein` does not appear.
- [ ] `dein-dein` is used only where lowercase is appropriate.
- [ ] The official primary logo file is used intact, legibly, and with clear
      space.
- [ ] Navy leads the brand treatment; gold is focused; plum is restrained.
- [ ] Typography is FK Grotesk Neue or an approved clean grotesk fallback.
- [ ] The asset feels useful, welcoming, energetic, trustworthy, and polished.
- [ ] The hierarchy is readable at the intended size and within platform-safe
      areas.
- [ ] There is one clear primary call to action.
- [ ] Dates, times, cities, venues, languages, URLs, availability, and prices
      match a trusted source exactly.
- [ ] Unknown facts were omitted rather than invented.
- [ ] Dein-Dein is not presented as the organizer, cinema, or ticket seller
      unless confirmed.
- [ ] All imagery, logos, music, footage, and likenesses have confirmed usage
      rights.
- [ ] Contrast, captions, and non-color cues make essential information
      accessible.
- [ ] The output has been checked for spelling, visual artifacts, unsafe
      content, and unintended claims.
